Privacy Policy

Last Updated: February 20, 2026

1. Information We Collect

VideoRepair is designed to process video files locally on our infrastructure. We do not collect or store:

  • Personal identification information
  • Login credentials or passwords
  • Private data outside of the provided video containers
  • User accounts or registration data

2. Video Data Processing

When you use VideoRepair:

  • Reference Video: We parse the healthy file's structure to extract atoms and codec parameters.
  • Damaged Video: We scan the corrupted file's media data (mdat) to match samples against the reference.
  • Local Execution: All repair logic via the Untrunc engine occurs in isolated temporary containers.

3. Data Storage & Retention

We do not maintain permanent copies:

  • Temporary Uploads: Files are stored in a secure temporary buffer during processing.
  • Auto-Deletion: Uploaded and repaired files are automatically purged from the server after the job is completed or whenever the session is cleared.
  • No Log Persistence: We do not store histories of filenames or video content.

4. Error Logging

We maintain minimal error logs to help troubleshoot engine failures. These logs:

  • Do not contain sensitive video data
  • Are accessible only to system administrators
  • Are purged every 30 days

5. Cookies

We use minimal functional cookies:

  • Session Identification: To manage your uploads during the current window.
  • No tracking or advertising cookies.

Disclaimer

1. Repair Accuracy

VideoRepair uses the Untrunc engine to reconstruct video containers. However:

  • Successful repair depends on the internal damage level of the record.
  • A matching reference video (same camera/settings) is required for best results.
  • Results may vary based on file format complexity (GoPro, Sony RSV, etc.).
